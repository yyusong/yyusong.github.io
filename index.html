About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Quantitative Marketing at the Stephen M. Ross School of Business, University of Michigan. My research centers on the economics of digitization, with a focus on platform strategies in e-commerce and content management on social media. My work covers a wide range of topics, including personalization, pricing, platform experimentation, user engagement, platform regulation, and product development. I use field experiments, structural modeling, causal inference, and advanced machine learning and artificial intelligence techniques to investigate consumer and supplier behaviors and inform managerial and policy decisions.
